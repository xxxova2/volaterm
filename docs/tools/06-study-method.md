# How to study with this terminal

**Deck:** A weekly loop that beats passive scrolling.  
**Level:** All

---

## The failure mode

People open twenty tabs, save zero notes, and “feel informed.”  
On Monday they cannot restate one mechanism.

This method is deliberately small.

---

## Weekly loop (90 minutes total)

### Monday — one mechanism (25 min)
Pick **one** Academy lesson. Read once without highlighting.  
Write five lines max:

1. Claim in plain English  
2. Mechanism  
3. What would falsify it  
4. Terminal screen that shows it  
5. One question you still have  

### Wednesday — screen time (25 min)
Open the desk for that lesson. Do not trade.  
Screenshot or jot: what does *today* look like vs the article’s base case?

### Friday — synthesis (20 min)
Re-read your Monday note. Edit lies.  
Write one paragraph: *This week, plumbing/vol/positioning said ___.*

### Optional — glossary quiz (10 min)
Cover definitions. Say them out loud. Painful = working.

---

## Note template (copy this)

```
Date:
Lesson:
One-line claim:
Mechanism:
Falsifier:
Terminal codes:
Today’s observation:
Open question:
```

---

## What not to do

- Do not binge ten lessons in one night.  
- Do not collect jargon without a falsifier.  
- Do not treat GEX or theta as moral virtues.  
- Do not skip funding because “I only trade SPX.”

---

## Success metric

After four weeks you can brief a skeptical friend in three minutes using **only** the three machines — no screenshots required.

That is the bar.
