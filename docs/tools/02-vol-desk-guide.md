# Vol Desk Guide: Surface, Smile, and Term

**Academy level:** Intermediate  
**Codes:** `SURF`, `SMILE`, `TERM`, `VOL`  
**What you will learn:** How to study the vol complex in-app; quality caveats; cross-links to Greeks and GEX.

---

## 1. Surface (`SURF` / `OVDV`)

**Job:** See IV across strikes and expiries as a landscape.

Study questions:

- Where is the high-IV ridge (events, short-dated stress)?  
- Are wings trustworthy or illiquid noise?  
- Does the surface agree with a calm or crisis macro day?

Use zoom and inspect tools when available. Prefer liquid strikes.

---

## 2. Smile (`SMILE` / `SKEW`)

**Job:** Fix one expiry; study skew and butterfly shape.

Study questions:

- Put wing vs call wing — what tail is expensive?  
- Did skew steepen today with spot down (classic) or not (unusual)?  
- How does this smile sit vs yesterday’s mental snapshot?

Pair with vanna education articles.

---

## 3. Term structure (`TERM`)

**Job:** ATM (or fixed delta) IV across dates.

Study questions:

- Contango or backwardation?  
- Event hump before CPI/FOMC/earnings?  
- Front rich vs back — is it 0DTE noise or multi-day stress?

---

## 4. Historical IV strips

When shown, compare **implied** to **realized/historical** proxies.

Study questions:

- Is the market charging a fat VRP or running cheap?  
- Adjust for known events before crowning “cheap vol.”

---

## 5. Quality / fit views

If a quality panel exists, use it to see where the surface is smooth vs broken. Educational point: **bad wings create fake trades** in the mind even if you never click send.

## 7. Key takeaways

1. Surface = landscape; smile = slice; term = time.  
2. Liquidity filters truth.  
3. Hist vs implied starts VRP study.  
4. Always cross-read positioning.  
5. Journal daily shape in one sentence.
