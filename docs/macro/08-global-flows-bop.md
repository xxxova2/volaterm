# Global Flows, Balance of Payments, and Macro Endgames


---

## 1. Flows dictate price (educational slogan)

Valuation frameworks (DCF, equity risk premium, term premium models) organize thought. In stress and in structural transitions, **who must buy or sell how much paper** often dominates short- and medium-term price.

This article connects plumbing and RV to the open-economy identities.

---

## 2. Balance of payments (BoP) identity

In accounting form (signs and textbook presentations vary slightly):

\[
\text{Current account} + \text{Capital/financial account} + \text{Errors} \approx 0
\]

**Current account:** trade in goods/services, primary/secondary income.  
**Financial account:** cross-border purchases of assets (bonds, equities, FDI, bank flows).

### U.S. cartoon
- Large current account deficits mean the U.S. consumes more than it produces in an accounting sense and must attract net capital inflows—or adjust.  
- Foreigners recycling dollars into Treasuries, agencies, equities, and deposits are part of that story.  
- Shocks that slow trade or weaponize finance can **slow recycling**, requiring higher yields or a weaker currency (or both) to clear.

Tariffs and trade wars are not only “inflation stories”; they are **BoP flow stories**.

---

## 3. Net international investment position (NIIP)

NIIP measures a country’s external assets minus liabilities. A deeply negative NIIP means large net debtor status.

### Why the U.S. is special (educational)
- Liabilities often denominated in USD.  
- External assets include foreign-currency claims.  
- A weaker dollar can **improve** the USD value of external assets while liabilities stay nominal—valuation effects cushion NIIP mechanics relative to classic EM sudden stops.

### The trap
If reserve managers and private foreigners reduce willingness to hold dollar claims (policy risk, fiscal concerns, alternatives), flows can reverse. That “sudden stop” language is EM history applied carefully to a reserve-currency center—with different plumbing, but not infinite patience.

## 4. Connecting issuance, dealers, and foreigners

Stack:

1. Fiscal deficit → Treasury **issuance**.  
2. Dealers intermediate under **SLR / G-SIB** constraints.  
3. Levered funds run **basis** and relative-value books financed in repo.  
4. Foreign official and private accounts absorb residual.  
5. If (2)–(4) jam, yields gap, vol rises, equities reprice risk.

This is the “endgame” framing of plumbing primers: not apocalypse theater, but **clearing mechanisms under constraint**.

## 5. Study method for flow days

1. What changed in trade/policy that affects dollar recycling?  
2. What does the auction calendar add to supply this week?  
3. Is private repo capacity healthy (SOFR−EFFR, RRP buffer)?  
4. Is equity vol confirming risk-off flow or ignoring it?  
5. Write the path of least resistance for **who is the marginal buyer of duration**.

---

## 6. Key takeaways

1. BoP identities force financing of deficits.  
2. Flows can dominate valuation for long stretches.  
3. NIIP valuation effects matter for the U.S., but are not a free pass.  
4. Issuance + dealer constraints + foreign demand = bond clearing.  
5. Cross-read FX, UST, and equity IV as one system.
