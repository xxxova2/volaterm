# Growth, inflation, liquidity — three numbers

**Deck:** Most macro noise collapses into G, I, and L.  
**Desk:** Rates / plumbing · multi-asset  
**Level:** Intermediate

![Growth · Inflation · Liquidity](/docs/academy/figures/gil-triangle.svg)

---

## Stop collecting narratives. Start sorting forces.

Every week produces a new story: soft landing, no landing, hard landing, pivot, fiscal, China, oil, elections.

You cannot trade twenty stories. You can track **three forces**:

| Letter | Force | Plain question |
|--------|-------|----------------|
| **G** | Growth | Is the economy heating, cooling, or rolling over? |
| **I** | Inflation | Are prices still sticky, cooling, or re-accelerating? |
| **L** | Liquidity | Is money and balance-sheet capacity expanding or contracting? |

Cross-asset prices are, roughly, the market’s vote on **which force dominates** and **who funds the risk**.

This is the same spine used by serious capital-flows writers (think Lyn Alden–style plumbing + flows, Joseph Wang–style Fed balance sheet, Macro Charts–style big picture): less hot take, more **which constraint binds**.

---

## Why liquidity is not optional

Growth and inflation get the TV time. **Liquidity** decides whether the system can hold the risk.

Liquidity is not one number. It is a bundle:

- Central bank balance sheets  
- Bank reserves and regulation  
- Overnight funding (repo, RRP, SOFR)  
- Foreign demand for Treasuries and FX basis  
- Private leverage capacity  

![Fed balance sheet (WALCL) — one liquidity window (FRED)](/docs/academy/figures/charts/walcl.png)

When L expands into a soft G and falling I, risk assets often party.  
When L contracts into sticky I, something usually breaks (vol, credit, or a basis trade).

---

## A practical weekly map

Ask only:

1. **G:** Is data surprising hot or cold vs consensus?  
2. **I:** Is the disinflation story intact?  
3. **L:** Are funding markets calm? Is the Fed (or peers) adding or draining?  
4. **Who is the marginal buyer** of duration / equities / credit this week?

If you cannot answer (4), you have a narrative, not a flow view.

---

## Rates are the transmission

The 10-year and the curve are not “the economy.” They are **prices of discounted cash flows + term premium + supply**.

![10-year Treasury yield (FRED)](/docs/academy/figures/charts/dgs10.png)

![2s10s curve (FRED)](/docs/academy/figures/charts/t10y2y.png)

Use them as **thermometers**, not prophecies:

- Rising yields with strong G and sticky I → different trade than  
- Rising yields with pure supply / term premium and soft G  

Same price move, different machine.

## Connect to the other desks

- **Options:** Liquidity shocks show up as vol of vol and skew.  
- **Positioning:** Forced hedging gets violent when funding and vol jump together.  
- **Terminal:** Rates plumbing desk first on stress days; then GEX; then size.

---

## Bottom line

> Sort the week into **Growth / Inflation / Liquidity**. Then ask who is funding the risk. Everything else is color commentary.
