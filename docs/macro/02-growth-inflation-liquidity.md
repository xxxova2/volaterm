## 1. Why three drivers, not one narrative

![Growth, Inflation, Liquidity triangle](/docs/academy/figures/gil-triangle.svg)

Media macro often collapses to a single story (“soft landing,” “hard landing,” “pivots”). Professional process keeps three state variables because **assets care about different mixtures**:

- Growth-sensitive equities can rise even if liquidity tightens—until they cannot.  
- Inflation-sensitive bonds can sell off while growth is fine.  
- Liquidity can dominate both when funding breaks.

---

## 2. Growth

**Concept:** real expansion of activity and earnings power.

**Market channels:**
- Equity earnings expectations and cyclicals vs defensives  
- Credit creation and default cycle  
- Commodity demand for industrial metals  
- Steepener/flattener impulses in the curve (growth vs recession pricing)

**Study habit:** track **levels and rates of change**. A still-high level of activity with deteriorating momentum is a different regime from a low level that is re-accelerating.

---

## 3. Inflation

**Concept:** change in the general price level and inflation expectations process.

**Market channels:**
- Nominal yields and real yields  
- Breakevens / inflation swaps  
- Equity multiple compression when inflation volatility rises  
- Commodity complex (partial, not identical)

**Study habit:** separate **goods vs services**, **sticky vs flexible**, and **level vs surprise**. Markets often trade the second derivative (disinflation vs re-acceleration) more than the year-over-year level alone.

---

## 4. Liquidity

**Concept:** the ease of obtaining leverage and the abundance of balance-sheet capacity. Not identical to “the Fed cut rates.”

Layers of liquidity:

| Layer | Examples |
|-------|----------|
| **Policy rates** | EFFR target, IORB |
| **Market funding** | SOFR, GC repo, FX swap, commercial paper |
| **Quantity / facilities** | Reserves, RRP, SRF usage, QT/QE |
| **Regulatory capacity** | SLR, G-SIB, dealer warehouse space |
| **Risk appetite** | Credit spreads, equity IV, basis dislocations |

Plumbing articles in this Academy detail the funding stack. Here the message is: **liquidity is multi-layered**; a cut in the policy rate does not automatically heal a dealer balance-sheet jam.

## 5. Regime collocation table (educational)

| Growth | Inflation | Liquidity | Illustrative cross-asset sketch |
|--------|-----------|-----------|----------------------------------|
| ↑ | ↓ | ↑ | Risk-on classic; bonds mixed |
| ↑ | ↑ | ↑ | Nominal growth; bonds pressured; equities uneven |
| ↓ | ↓ | ↓ | Deflationary stress; flight to quality if system holds |
| ↓ | ↑ | ↓ | Stagflationary pain; correlations hostile |
| ↑ | ↓ | ↓ | Late-cycle squeeze risk if liquidity bites |

These are **cartoons for thinking**, not trade signals. Always overlay positioning.

## 6. Mapping to this app’s desks

| Driver | Primary desk | Secondary |
|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Growth | Macro panel, equity vol context | Sector/beta tools if present |
| Inflation | UST curves, global yields, STIR | Commodities via external study |
| Liquidity | Plumbing barometer, SOFR−EFFR, RRP, basis | FX board, cash–futures monitor |

**Vol** is the price of uncertainty about all three. When liquidity is the binding constraint, equity IV and credit often reprice together.

---

## 7. Common mistakes

1. Treating CPI as the only inflation object (ignore wages, shelter lags, expectations).  
2. Treating “liquidity” as M2 only (ignore dealer capacity and collateral).  
3. Ignoring that **positioning** can invert the sign of a “good” print.  
4. Using stock–bond correlation from the last decade as eternal truth.  
5. Forgetting that options markets price **paths**, not only terminal growth outcomes.

---

## 8. Daily study checklist

1. G: one growth pulse (labor or activity).  
2. I: one inflation pulse (print or market-implied).  
3. L: plumbing barometer (SOFR−EFFR, RRP, reserves regime).  
4. Vol: ATM IV term + skew direction of change.  
5. Positioning: GEX sign / walls for the index or name you study.  
6. One sentence: “Today the binding constraint is ___.”

---

## 9. Key takeaways

1. G/I/L is a state vector, not a slogan.  
2. Liquidity includes balance-sheet and collateral, not only the policy rate.  
3. Regimes change correlations.  
4. Vol and credit are early telltales of liquidity stress.  
5. Checklist beats narrative.
