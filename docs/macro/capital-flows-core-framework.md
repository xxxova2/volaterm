# Capital Flows — Core Macro Framework (Research Synthesis)

## 1. How To Think About Markets, Trading, and Wealth Management

- Wealth management = mapping future liabilities and building a portfolio durable across **all** economic/market regimes (nominal and real).
- Three operating modes: **Innovate → Systematize → Run**, iteratively (laboratory-like business). Adaptability to regime change is the survival requirement.
- 20-yr tailwinds (globalization, USD reserve status, easy Fed, demographics) are fading; 2022 showed stocks AND bonds can fall together. Active management matters more going forward.
- The three macro drivers of every asset: **Growth, Inflation, Liquidity**.
- Goal: quantify G/I/L dynamically, map asset sensitivity, then apply market timing + risk management. Prometheus Research cited for regime collocation backtests.

## 2. Information Age, Thinking Preemptively and Market Update

- Information quality > information quantity. Knowing *what is priced in* matters more than knowing a data point early — reaction depends on net positioning.
- Read history for timeless variables; use **redundancy planning / scenario analysis** (German General Staff model: pre-written orders for multiple scenarios). View future as a distribution, not deterministic.
- 2023 setup: watch the **LEVEL of Core CPI** and the **LEVEL of unemployment/labor market**. Choppy, stop-and-go forward curve until one breaks decisively.
- Signals: ES/NQ ratio vs rates; cash at ~4% is a high hurdle rate; fade unrealistic over-extrapolations.

## 3. Information Broker/Alpha Generation

- **Communication problem**: experts skip embedded causal assumptions; newcomers must extract them by asking good questions. Become a broker of information/insight.
- **Alpha** = excess return over benchmark; peak performance = absolute positive returns in every regime AND outperformance. Weigh opportunity cost of being in cash vs. crash-catching.
- Relentless execution (Tim Grover framing) over raw intelligence.

## 4. Research Drop + Framework

- World = a flow chart of capital. Build a **Country Profile** (history, demographics, resource/geography, political/monetary/fiscal regime) then connect to **Economic Data** (flows: GDP/consumption/investment/net exports; capital structure: household/corporate/financial/sovereign balance sheets). Watch quarterly/monthly/weekly frequencies; Integrated Macroeconomic Accounts (FRED) is the overlooked dataset.
- Bridge to **Asset Markets**: equities (earnings/valuation + cross-sectional factors), fixed income (credit + duration, STIR/bills/notes/bonds), commodities, FX (BoP, cross-border flows, reserves, XCCY).
- **Expectations vs Actual**: economic surprise indices, MoM/3-mo trend, earnings surprise (EPS/sales). Structural dynamics set the probable distribution/skew.
- **Positioning & Microstructure**: financial plumbing, COT, option positioning, ETF/mutual/foreign flows, active/passive/systematic share, autocorrelation. Microstructure: intraday/Globex session, seasonality, order imbalances, liquidity, limit order book.
- Resources: Concoda (repo/plumbing visuals), Joseph Wang / FedGuy (central banking), Michael Howell / CrossBorder Capital (global liquidity), WifeyAlpha (intraday research).

## 5. Model for Markets

- Connect every asset to its economic data (auto sales→auto stocks, retail sales→retail stocks, inflation→bonds, industrial production→industrials). Aggregate by sector + sensitivity.
- **Equities**: (1) earnings vs valuation function (2022 was valuation contraction, not earnings cuts); (2) cross-sectional fundamentals + momentum across S&P sectors; (3) factor exposure per regime.
- **Fixed income**: always know **duration** and **credit**. If all bonds sell off but credit spreads don't widen, credit risk is constant.
- **Commodities**: top-down GIP pricing + bottom-up supply/demand per commodity; inflation ≠ commodities.
- **FX**: gateway into a country's goods/services/assets; decompose via Balance of Payments drivers.
- Best trades = identify points where the market prices something *unrealistic* and fade it — thesis via **disconfirmation** ("x cannot do y"), not confirmation. Taleb/Popper/Soros framing.

## 6. Positioning and Microstructure

- **Financial plumbing**: how liquidity/collateral transform across balance sheets (Concoda, FedGuy, CrossBorder Capital).
- **COT positioning**: long-term trend, 3-mo trend, weekly change; Z-score/ROC. More one-sided → smaller squeeze to move price (SVB: crowded short-end squeeze).
- **Option positioning**: opex calendar (inflection points cluster on large opex), open interest/volume + gamma hedging, implied-vol premium/discount, vol term structure, skew (tails). Watch CVOL, T1alpha, Mike Green, Roaring Meows.
- **Flows**: ETF/mutual/foreign investment flows; contradictions vs COT are informative (e.g., Japan foreign bond buying vs UST/Yen).
- **Active/Passive/Systematic**: share of market + constraints create characteristic price action (D.E. Shaw dispersion paper).
- **Microstructure**: intraday volume/VWAP vs Globex (Asia/London/US) sessions, seasonality (know the *why*), order imbalance/elasticity/LOB. Book: *Trades, Quotes and Prices* (Bouchaud et al.).

## 7. Macro Alpha Primer: Credit Risk and Duration Risk

- All variables organized as **Growth, Inflation, Liquidity**. These map to assets via **credit risk** + **duration risk**.
  - **Duration risk**: sensitivity of PV of cash flows to rates (longer duration = more rate-sensitive).
  - **Credit risk**: uncertainty of receiving promised cash flows (default probability).
- Four regimes: duration↑/credit↑ (worst for bonds), duration↑/credit↓ (partial offset), duration↓/credit↑, duration↓/credit↓ (best).
- Map **nominal vs real GDP** (level, rate of change, spread) → credit/duration regimes. 2020 HY drawdown was credit-driven; 2022 was duration-driven.
- Temporally dependent: Z-scores across timeframes reveal *types* of moves. Inflection signals = shifts across multiple variables + changing correlations + nonlinear moves.

## 8. Macro Alpha Primer: Correlations

- Outright + relative relationships both signal; correlations drive portfolio P&L (2022 double drawdown worse than 2020 offset).
- The 8 G/I/L scenario collocations determine stock-bond correlation (a "three-body problem" — chaotic, initial conditions matter).
- Same input driving the same attribution in both assets = correlation exists. Map via S&P500 + rates primers.
- 2020: negative stock-bond corr (deflation/recession). 2022→2024: positive corr (negative liquidity impulse / Goldilocks).
- Vol-control portfolios forced to sell both in 2022 → amplified. Trades: long/short stocks AND bonds, vol-weighted, for optionality across futures.

## 9. Macro Alpha Primer: Macro Catalysts, Hedging Pressure, Positioning

- Economic data is **not** lagging if used to project the future (path dependency: future is a derivative of present). Model level/rate-of-change/base effects.
- Markets price an **implied future** (inflation swaps settle at CPI, FF futures at realized Fed Funds, IV at realized vol). Information spectrum: uncertainty → certainty as data prints.
- **Hedging pressure**: managers concentrate hedges around catalysts with weighted significance (CPI, NFP, FOMC, Jackson Hole) especially when they lack an edge there.
- **Positioning** determines how prices react as info crosses the spectrum. If a recession is perfectly priced, the print doesn't move markets; if wrong, repricing.
- CPI case study: 2022 inflation prints = equity bottoms (Fed-hiking logic); 2023 net-short positioning + inflation prints = mechanical unwinds higher.

## 10. Macro Alpha Primer: Positioning Premiums

- Spot and options both price future probabilities; options isolate causality via the **Greeks** (Δ, Γ, Vega, Θ, Rho).
- Key option signals: **IV vs realized vol** (premium/discount), **skew** (tail pricing), **term structure**, **notional/OI/volume**, **catalyst (opex) dates**.
- These connect to spot: Aug-2024 mini-liquidation — selling pulled spot from expectations, IV premium + skew blew out, term structure shifted. Always know the **WHY** (asset correlation context) before fading.
- Map correlations of IV, vol premium/discount, skew, term structure (e.g., VIX vs MOVE) — shifts in vol correlations often precede spot correlation changes.
- Tools: CME CVOL/Quikstrike, CBOE indices on TradingView, Menthor Q, VolSignals.

## 11. Macro Alpha Primer: Stacking Edges To Identify a Sucker (Synthesis)

- **Idea 1**: Quantify fundamentals + momentum/mean-reversion + correlations + positioning to find when an agent **misread the system and is constrained to unwind**. Edge = identifying the "sucker."
- **Idea 2**: Match your **patience** to the market's **impatience** (visible in premiums/discounts they pay). Different time preference + risk tolerance required to take the bet.
- **Stacking edges** ("pages in the book"): multiple reasons for a trade → positive asymmetry across outcomes. Constantly ask: is this the *best* trade now and how does it fit the portfolio of bets?
- Process = a business; AI can run the spreadsheet/models. "Clear thinker" > smart.

## Recurring Themes (useful for terminal design)

- Everything is connected; no asset class or data point in a silo.
- Macro prism = Growth / Inflation / Liquidity → credit risk + duration risk → asset attribution.
- Markets price an *implied future*; track expectations-vs-actual and how info moves uncertainty→certainty.
- Positioning + option premiums reveal *who is wrong / impatient* → fade unrealistic pricing.
- Best trades are disconfirmation-based fades of unrealistic market pricing, with stacked edges and defined patience.
