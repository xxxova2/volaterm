# How this Academy works

**Read time:** ~5 minutes  
**Who this is for:** Anyone who can follow a market day and wants the *desk* version — not a tip sheet, not a 400-page textbook.

![Curriculum map](/docs/academy/figures/curriculum-map.svg)

---

## What Academy is (and is not)

**Academy** is education: full essays on **options / greeks** and **macro / rates plumbing**. Black terminal chrome, white type, wide enough to read.

**Positioning** is **not** an Academy track. GEX, walls, charm, dealer mode, book tools — those belong on the **Positioning desk** as Bloomberg-style terminal product. You *build and use* them in the app; you do not study them as a third curriculum here.

---

## The problem this fixes

Most “options education” is either:

- **Tip culture** — screenshots, emojis, zero mechanics, or  
- **Textbook sludge** — formulas first, intuition never.

Professional desks talk about **funding** and **how P&L actually decomposes**. This Academy pulls those conversations **into the terminal** as full articles.

---

## Two tracks

### 1. Options & Greeks
Why short premium is risk compensation, how P&L splits (theta, delta, vega, gamma, vanna, volga), surfaces, 0DTE, Black–Scholes intuition.

**First lesson:** *The theta income trap*

### 2. Macro & Rates
Why risk assets can be leveraged at all: growth / inflation / liquidity, overnight rates, RRP, basis, swaps, FX basis. Real charts from public data where we can.

**First lesson:** *Growth, inflation, liquidity — three numbers*

## What “done” looks like

On a random session you can answer, in plain language:

- Where is overnight funding sitting (tight / easy / weird)?  
- Is vol rich or cheap relative to recent realized, and *why might that be wrong*?  
- How does today’s P&L actually decompose if you are long/short gamma or vega?

For **who must buy or sell stock when price moves** — open the **Positioning** tab and work the desk tools. That is product, not homework.
